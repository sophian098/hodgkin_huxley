Hodgkin-Huxley Model Equations

Mathematical framework for action potential generation

Membrane Potential Dynamics

dV/dt = (1/Cm) · [I - gNam3h(V - ENa) - gKn4(V - EK) - gL(V - EL)]

Gating Variable Dynamics

Potassium (n)
dn/dt = αn(V)(1-n) - βn(V)n
Sodium activation (m)
dm/dt = αm(V)(1-m) - βm(V)m
Sodium inactivation (h)
dh/dt = αh(V)(1-h) - βh(V)h

Rate Functions

αn = 0.01(V+55) / [1 - exp(-(V+55)/10)]
βn = 0.125 · exp(-(V+65)/80)
αm = 0.1(V+40) / [1 - exp(-(V+40)/10)]
βm = 4.0 · exp(-(V+65)/18)
αh = 0.07 · exp(-(V+65)/20)
βh = 1 / [1 + exp(-(V+35)/10)]

Model Interpretation

Biological meaning of the mathematical terms

dV/dt — Membrane Potential Change

Describes how the membrane voltage changes over time as a function of applied current and the three ionic currents (Na+, K+, leak). The capacitive membrane acts as an electrical circuit integrating all currents.

IK — Potassium Current

Potassium conductance as a function of membrane voltage. The n^4 term represents four identical activation gates that must all be open for K+ to flow. K+ current is responsible for repolarization, bringing the membrane back to resting potential.

n: K+ activation gate (x4) — opens slowly with depolarization, closes slowly

INa — Sodium Current

Sodium conductance controlled by two distinct gates. The m^3h term reflects three activation gates (m) and one inactivation gate (h). Na+ influx causes the rapid upstroke of the action potential.

m: Na+ activation gate (x3) — opens rapidly in response to voltage increase
h: Na+ inactivation gate (x1) — closes with sustained depolarization
Threshold: ≈ -55 mV. Below this, the stimulus is subthreshold and no action potential occurs. Once threshold is reached, a full spike is generated (all-or-none).
